# Lore about my learning journey

This is a collection of insights and lessons learned from my learning journey thus far.
It serves as explanations for why I've made certain decisions and what I've learned along the way.

## I've learned a lot, but did one crucial beginner mistake as mentioned [here](https://github.com/Neonsy/CSharp-Pong/pull/1#issuecomment-2155821149)

> In the digital realm of bits and bytes,
> A junior dev pursued new heights.
>
> Eager, bright, with skills to gain,
> But missed a step, his profile plain.
>
> He coded projects late at night,
> Learning heaps, a future bright.
>
> But one small flaw, he failed to see,
> No repos shared, lost visibility.
>
> His profile bare, no work to show,
> Skills he had, but who would know?
>
> A poor reflection, sadly seen,
> Though his knowledge was quite keen.
>
> So let this be a lesson, friend,
> Show your work from start to end.
>
> Create repos, make them shine,
> Let your profile be your sign.

> [!NOTE]
> Having the potential to learn quickly is only half the battle.
> 
> The real value comes from sharing what one has learned.
> 
> Things like for example HTML and CSS might seem trivial to a lot of people, but it's important to keep in mind that diving into a new area feels like a challenge at first.
> 
> I'll strive to share as much of what I learn as possible (if it makes sense to make a Repo out of it that is).

## What happened to some of my C# Repos?

> In the beginning, there was Git,
> And GitHub, where developers commit.
> 
> A junior dev, eager and bright,
> Created an account, felt it was right.
> 
> No clue how to start or begin,
> Git commands seemed hard to win.
> 
> Before gaining the needed knack,
> He switched to [Codeberg](https://codeberg.org/), looking back.
> 
> The reason for switching, to be clear,
> Was the appeal of [Gitea](https://gitea.io/), drawing near.
> 
> Focused on C#, his primary game,
> Visual Studio was his mainframe.
> 
> Using GUI, he thought he’d be slick,
> But moving repos manually was quite the trick.
> 
> Efforts led to a tangled mess,
> Projects scrambled, caused distress.
> 
> So here’s a lesson, clear and true:
> Learn your tools, they’ll see you through!
> 
> Eventually, he returned to GitHub’s might,
> With newfound wisdom, his path was right.

> [!NOTE]
> I was so focused on learning C#, that I gambled on the Git GUI in VS being enough. Now I know better.
>
> This is why some of my C# Repos are either rebased or reworded, loosing the detailed commit history.

> [!NOTE]
> It's not that I don't like the CLI, on the contrary, I love it ever since I forced myself to get into it all the way back in 2018.
>
> It was around that time, besides already using the CMD and later the PowerShell on several occasions, that I dove into the Ubuntu Server in order to get acquainted with the commands, permissions and basic setup.
>
> I just had a hard time starting to get into Git, so I postponed the learning on that topic, which I shouldn't have done.
